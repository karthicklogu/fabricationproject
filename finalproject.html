What is Fabrication?

Fabrication is the process of constructing raw metal material into usable products through cutting, shaping, bending, assembling and finishing

For the most part, metal fabrication shops are centralized metalworking operations which concentrate many different metalworking processes in one locale in order to create finished metal products. For example, it is widespread for metal fabrication shops to employ various metallurgical specialists (e.g. a welder, a blacksmith, a CNC specialist) in order to perform their various projects. Among the most commonly employed metalworking processes at various stages of a metal fabrication project are casting, bending, stamping, welding, punching, CNC machining, and forging. Although metal fabrication is commonly confused with welding, the two are not equivalent; rather, welding is a common step in metal fabrication as a whole.
